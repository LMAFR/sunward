# Sunward — Story Bible

The game follows the *beat structure* of Golden Sun 1 as scaffolding, with
all names, prose, and dialogue original. Over time the structure itself
will drift (new locations, characters, rewritten arcs) until the source is
only a reminiscence. **Hard rule: no Nintendo/Camelot names, text, or
assets, ever.** This file is the naming + structure authority; keep it in
sync with content.

## Cast — with inherited narrative theses

These theses come from the user's ROM-hack design work
(`vault/projects/golden-sun-mod/design/`) and are canon here from day one.

| Character | Role (GS1 analog) | Thesis |
|---|---|---|
| **Aren** | Protagonist (Isaac) | Coming-of-age: starts uncertain, finds his voice across the journey; never a blank slate |
| **Bram** | Loyal friend (Garet) | Loud where Aren is quiet; courage that outruns judgment, slowly maturing |
| **Liora** | Friend, sister of the lost (Jenna) | Grief worn as anger; the first to believe Caled lives |
| **Caled** | Lost brother (Felix) | The weathered brother: saved by people who now own his compliance; says little because everything he could say endangers someone |
| **Maester Edrin** | Scholar (Kraden) | The scholar: curiosity that borders on culpability; his need to *know* helped cause the breach |
| **Vask & Maere** | Antagonist pair (Saturos/Menardi) | Not villains: envoys of a dying northern homeland that needs the Beacons lit to survive |
| **Cyrel** | The schemer (Alex) | The sick genius: a brilliant water-Attuned whose body cannot sustain his gifts; wants agency over his own power, not dominion; running out of time |
| **Sela** | Healer guardian (Mia) | Clan guardian: last keeper of a duty everyone else abandoned |
| **The Warden** | Guardian (Wise One) | The Test: its instructions are themselves a trial; it never tells the whole truth, to see what the bearer does with half of it |

## Central tension (inherited from the theses)

Nobody in this story is operating on complete information, and the two
sides are both *right*: the north dies if the Beacons stay dark; the world
risks ruin if they are lit carelessly. The Warden knows more than it says
and says less than it should — deliberately. "Better written" means every
faction's action is defensible from inside their knowledge.

## Act 1 — beats (current playable scope in **bold**)

1. *Prologue (3 years past):* a storm breaks the mountainside; a rockfall
   takes part of Aldera; Caled and his parents are lost at the flooded
   river. The village believes the mountain's wards are weakening.
2. **Present day: Elder Thane asks Aren to escort Maester Edrin's errand —
   read the wards at the Aurin Shrine.**
3. **At the Shrine: the seal is already broken. Three of the four Anima
   Stones are gone — taken north. Among the raiders' traces, signs that
   one of them walks like Caled. The earth stone alone remains.**
4. **The Warden stirs and sets its first (half-true) charge: the stones
   must not reach the Beacons.** *(Seed of the Test.)*
5. Aren, Bram, Liora and Edrin leave Aldera in pursuit. End of Act 1.

## Writing rules

- Dialogue is original prose. Beat *structure* may echo GS1; sentences
  never do.
- Each named character speaks per their thesis voice profile.
- The Warden never lies outright; it omits. Mark omissions in design
  notes so later acts can pay them off.
- Drift policy: when we modify a beat away from the GS1 skeleton, update
  this file and note the divergence — divergences are the long-term goal.
